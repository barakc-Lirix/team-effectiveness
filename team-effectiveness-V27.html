Welcome & Instructions

This questionnaire is based on Patrick Lencioni's model. It helps identify where the team is strong and where improvement is needed.

The Five Dysfunctions of a Team by Patrick Lencioni presents a pyramid model of five interrelated challenges that undermine team effectiveness: absence of trust, fear of conflict, lack of commitment, avoidance of accountability, and inattention to results. The model starts with trust at the base, emphasizing that without vulnerability-based trust, teams struggle to engage in healthy conflict, make aligned decisions, or stay focused on shared results. Each level depends on the strength of the one below, making the model a clear and actionable framework for diagnosing team dynamics.

Inattention to Results

The pursuit of individual goals and personal status erodes the focus on collective success.

Avoidance of Accountability

The need to avoid interpersonal discomfort prevents team members from holding one another accountable for their behaviors and performance.

Lack of Commitment

The lack of clarity or buy-in prevents team members from making decisions they will stick to.

Fear of Conflict

The desire to preserve artificial harmony stifles the occurrence of productive, ideological conflict.

Absence of Trust

The fear of being vulnerable with team members prevents the building of trust within the team.

Questionnaire (15 items)

How to Complete:

Read each statement and honestly rate how it applies to your team. Don't overthink your answers—your first instinct is usually most accurate.

This assessment takes about 5 minutes and will provide immediate, personalized insights into your team's strengths and areas for growth.

Remember: Every team has areas to improve. This tool is designed to illuminate opportunities, not to judge or criticize.
